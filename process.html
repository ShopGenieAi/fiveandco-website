How We Work

Our Process

From first conversation to live website, here's exactly what working with Five&Co looks like. No surprises, no runaround — just a clear, professional process that gets you online fast.

Average project timeline: 2–4 weeks.

Start Your Project
01
Discovery
Brief & Goals
We kick off with a conversation — your business, your goals, your audience, and what success looks like for your website. We collect brand assets, agree on a site map, and make sure we're 100% aligned before a single line of code gets written.
02
Design
Visual Direction
A visual mockup is created to show you the layout, colour palette, and design direction. You sign off before we build. This means no surprises — and no costly back-and-forth once development begins.
03
Build
Full Site Development
The fun bit. Your site is hand-coded — HTML, CSS, JavaScript. Content is integrated, mobile optimisation is done, and the technical SEO foundation is set up. We target 90+ Google PageSpeed scores as a baseline, not a stretch goal.
04
Review
Round One Feedback
You review the full site and send through your first round of consolidated feedback. We make the amendments. Two revision rounds are included in the build fee — we keep it clean and efficient.
05
Revise
Final Polish
Round two. Final tweaks, copy updates, last-minute refinements. This is where we button everything up and make sure you're 100% happy before we go live.
06
Launch
Go Live
Domain connected, DNS configured, Vercel deployment done, SSL live. Your site is up and running. We handle everything — you just need to tell people about it.
07
Handover
Client Walkthrough
We walk you through the live site, show you how Google Analytics and Search Console work, and make sure you know what's what. No black boxes — full transparency on everything we've built.
08
Ongoing
Hosting & Support
Monthly hosting, maintenance, and support kicks in at $30/month. Additional changes are billed hourly at $75–$100/hr. Your site stays fast, secure, and up-to-date — without you having to lift a finger.
